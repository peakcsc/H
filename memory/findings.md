## Confirmed Color Palette for Native Screens
- Primary: Deep teal `#1A6B8A` (pool/water/outdoor)
- Secondary: Fresh green `#3A7D44` ("native" flora — unique brand gap)
- CTA Accent: Warm amber `#F5A623` (high-contrast buttons)
- Base: Off-white `#F8F9FA`
- Text: Charcoal `#1C2B33`
- No competitor owns green+teal — this is a visual gap we claim

## Confirmed Hero Headline
"50 Years of Protecting South Florida Pools & Patios. One Family. One Phone Call."

## Key Success Patterns (must-haves)
1. Sticky header — phone number always visible, top-right
2. Hero = headline + trust hook + CTA button + real photo backdrop
3. Visual service CARDS (not text list) — icon + name + 2-line description
4. Google Review widget embedded on homepage (Trustindex/Elfsight)
5. "Why choose us" with icon badges
6. Trust badge row: BBB / Google 5-star / licensed / years in business
7. Gallery with real job photos
8. City-specific service area section
9. Google Maps embed
10. Contractor license number displayed

## Native Screens Competitive Advantages
1. 50+ years in Broward County — no competitor matches this
2. Full service stack (rescreens + enclosures + aluminum roofs + super gutters)
3. True Broward County native — brand name + history = brand platform
4. 954 area code signals local presence

## What Weak Sites Do (avoid all of these)
- No mobile optimization
- Phone buried in footer only
- Stock photography
- Services in a paragraph (not cards)
- No reviews displayed
- No Google Maps embed
- Slow load from uncompressed images
- Unclear service area
- No license number

## Typography Recommendation
- Font: Inter or DM Sans (Google Fonts, free)
- Rounded CTA buttons
- Mobile-first layout
